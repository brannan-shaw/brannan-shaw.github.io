Emotional Intelligence
What is Emotional Intelligence?
Emotional intelligence, sometimes referred to as EQ (Emotional Quotient), is the skill of reading your own and other people's feelings and emotions and using that information to make decisions.
It allows someone to look internally and acknowledge an emotion without letting it control them.
What is the difference between EQ and IQ?
EQ is people based. It is rooted in perceiving and understanding the way that a person is feeling or acting. It is honed through the inspection and reflection of inter/intrapersonal interactions.

IQ is logic based. It is quantified by a person’s ability to rationalize data and solve problems.
Why is EQ important?
Emotional intelligence is important as it affects the way that we approach situations. An emotionally intelligent person is someone who is able to recognise an emotion within themselves or another person and, acknowledging that, adapt their approach to better facilitate the situation.
It allows people in leadership roles to manage much more effectively due to the ability to understand the wants of each person in their team and how they can be catered to.
