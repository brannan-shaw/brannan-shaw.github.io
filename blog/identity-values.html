My Identity & Values
Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision.
Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.
My current job involves dealing with independent contractors and resolving issues that come up anywhere in their end of the pipeline. I feel that the attitude normally taken towards contractors can be quite apathetic towards the fact that they are still people. There have been times where we are expected to take certain approaches when dealing with said contractors and I adapt my own approach as I cannot, with a good conscience, tell these people that they should do something that I do not always believe that they should have to do.
Reflecting on it, I am glad that I have upheld my own beliefs and values even if it means that I may not progress within the company due to my approach.
Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.
The values that I now hold dear have been imprinted on me from everywhere.
My parents emboldened my drive for knowledge and answers, pitting me against my siblings to see who could find the answer to a question first.
My Grandma demonstrated the need to show love and care for not just those close to us, but everyone that you interact with.
Moving to and living in New Zealand has taught me to work hard, but at the end of the day, still find joy in your surroundings.
The people I have worked alongside have made me appreciate the necessity of compassion and teamwork.
Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.
I am logical, hard-working, and curious. When I am given a task, I obtain any and all relative information that I can, then I strive to complete it to the best of my ability.
The effect that this has on my learning is that I am always looking for how to learn and fully understand the next thing. I love to learn and will push myself to reach a functional understanding of whatever task that I am set.
Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?
I find it hard to stay on topic/task at times. If I am researching a subject, I will often lead myself down a rabbit hole of certain aspects that I find an interest in. I also struggle with creativity at times. I am able to see and work on variabilities within situations but it is a challenge to start something from scratch.
Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.
Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.
When I was working at the supermarket as a Duty Manager, I was often working the later shifts alongside mostly younger staff (students working after school).
A lot of the time, there was a fair amount of lost productivity due to chatting and an inability to multi-task. The other managers (who were older than I) often took a hard approach to this, separating or giving out less desirable tasks if they were talking too much. This meant that quite a few of the managers were treated almost fearfully or talked about behind their backs.
I had a different attitude. I knew that they would want to talk regardless and would end up wasting time out of sight if I assigned them other tasks. So I set time goals or made it a competition between some of them. I told them that they could work together and talk as much as they wanted as long as a task was completed efficiently. I would set them a time limit and basically bet a nicer task vs a less desirable one contingent on if they beat the time. I got comments from other supervisors and managers about how they did not know that some of those staff could work so well.
I still feel that this was a good strategy. I wanted people to be able to get even a little enjoyment whilst in the workplace where a lot of the time it could be quite grueling, thankless work.
