Foundations Reflections
What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I have learnt to take a step back at times and consider an approach from another direction. My ego would sometimes convince me that I must be doing the right thing from the start.
I have also realised that people are not going to judge when you do not know something. In the past I have sometimes been apprehensive to ask for help in a public forum for fear of judgement, but realising that my classmates are pretty much in the same boat as me and that we are all here to learn has made a massive difference.
Thirdly, to truly learn something, I am going to have to repeat it a lot. I am not going to remember every tidbit from the get go.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

They help us apply ourselves effectively. We are not able to learn without working hard and dedicating ourselves to it. We need to respect the content that we are learning and the time and energy put in by those to help us learn.
Empathy gives us a better platform to program from. If we approach a task with the mmindset that other people are going to have to understand it aswell, we can factor that in to how we want our final product to appear / work.
Self-awareness keeps us accountable. It allows us to incorporate our values and empathy into our actions and ensure the best outcome through those.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Honestly, how much it has actually worked. Quite often, I am a skeptic, but having seen the difference some of the things we have learnt about have made, I feel more willing to try more things going forward.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

The self reflection. I am very bad at looking inwards and analysing myself fairly. At times I am far too critical, and others I am to prideful, so finding a balance and taking note of both in the same space has been tough.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Everywhere you work, you are going to be interacting with people. Being better prepared to approach situations with said people or situations that call for self improvement is invaluable.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not at all. While it is sometimes more exciting to just get on with the code, being given the skills to better myself and make my own learning easier has made the time 100% worth it.
