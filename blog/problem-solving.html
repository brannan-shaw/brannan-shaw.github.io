Problem Solving
A simple problem that I hit a block on

I got stuck finding the solution to the Capitalize challenge in the Javascript Kata.
Replacing the first letter of each word using the map method. This method being new to me, I got a basic understanding from looking it up before charging straight in to try a solution. This onbly made me frustrated.
I had to take a step back. Put more time in to learning about the method and ways in which it is applied. Being able to talk myself through an example helped me wrap my head around the approach I needed to take and which variables fit in where.
I found that frustration that is felt through the whole process turns into elation so quickly when the right solution is found and that is what kept me going.
I've learned that I need to put more research into an unknown factor before applying it and that breaking an issue down to its roots helps me understand my path to the solution a lot better.

A time I elegantly solved a problem

I cannot think of an issue which I have elegantly solved. With the problems that I have been facing in these challenges, I feel that I have blundered my way through them a fair bit and occasionaly I stumble upon the correct answer on the first try.
Despite this, I learn from each problem that I face, using it to understand how I can better aproach any issues in the future.

Confidence in Problem Solving techniques
  • Pseudocode- Pseudocode is something that I just need to get in the habit of doing. At this point I always forget about it until I have worked through and solved the issue then realised how useful it would have been through that process
  • Trying Something- I often start with just trying something. Sometimes to my detriment due to not fully understanding a concept first
  • Rubber Ducky Method- I think I just do this naturally. I talk to myself the whole time that I am trying to solve a problem
  • Reading Error Messages- I often read error messages but at times I feel I do not get enough detail from them
  • Console.logging- It is not something that I am in the habit of doing but the few times that I have used it, it helped a lot
  • Googling- Do it none stop
  • Asking your peers for help- I haven't reached a point yet where I feel comfortable(?) going directly to my peers for help. I hope for that to change as we interact more often
  • Asking coaches for help- I will often do this as a last resort, and/or if there is something within my code that worked but I do not understand
  • Improving your process with reflection- This is a process that I am working on and already starting to see imnprovement within myself from
