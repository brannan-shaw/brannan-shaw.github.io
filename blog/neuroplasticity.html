Neuroplasticity and the Growth Mindset
What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the brain's ability to change and adapt in response to experience. For a long time, it was believed that the brain stopped developing after a certain age. We now know this isn't true. The brain remains flexible, constantly remapping based on what we do, think, and learn

It is a bit like when you walk on a nature trail. The more a certain path is taken, the more defined it becomes. Our brains work the same way. The more you practice a skill or challenge yourself with new things, the wider and more efficient those pathways in your brain will become.

What is a Growth Mindset?

A growth mindset is the belief and approach that intelligence and abilities can be developed through effort, learning, and persistence.
In contrast, a fixed mindset is where people believe that their traits and attributes are static and unchangeable.

Some thoughts driven by a growth mindset might be; "I cannot do this yet, but I will if I keep practicing." or "This failure is a learning opportunity."

Influence on learning

When you combine these two concepts, your ability to learn and improve yourself will evolve.

  • Challenges will start to look opportunities instead of obstacles.
  • You will become more resilient wheen facing failure.

A personal example of this is my thoughts towards loops in Javascript. I was overwhelmed when I first learned them and tried to apply them. I started to think that if I was struggling with this, what else am I going to struggle with? I had to stop myself.
I applied that growth mindset, acknowledging that, while it may be difficult now, I will be able to master them if I persist.
